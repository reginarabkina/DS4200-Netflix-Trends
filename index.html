ANALYZING NETFLIX TRENDS THROUGH IMDb SCORES & ENTERTAINMENT CHARACTERISTICS

Introduction

Netflix once dominated the global streaming industry, but now numerous platforms compete for viewers' attention. Streaming has become the new norm in a way, as it begins to replace cable television, with channels moving content to their own platforms. New competitors such as Max, Peacock, Amazon Prime Video, Hulu, and many more have entered the streaming arena. While Netflix still holds power in its respective industry, how has the application changed across the years? With new competition, it becomes clear that the platform must continue expanding its library as it has done in the past, but does this mean sacrificing quality in exchange for quantity? Additionally, does it cater to the same audience it always has, or has that shifted as time has progressed? Our project aims to analyze these questions, as well as many more, regarding Netflix and how it has adapted with time.

Reference Papers

To learn more, you can visit the article Netflix, Library Analysis, and Globalization: Rethinking Mass Media Flows, which examines Netflix's 2021 data, revealing strategies like offering binge-worthy shows and releasing content in multiple languages to appeal to a global audience. Another article,“We Don’t Aspire to Be Netflix”, explores Netflix’s growth over time, highlighting its expansion to more countries, growing viewer bases, and use of user data for tailored recommendations. Both papers offer valuable insights into Netflix's evolving strategies, providing context for our analysis of Netflix trends and the changes observed in the data.

Introduction to the Data

For our analysis, we utilize two datasets from Kaggle: Netflix IMDB Scores and Netflix Movies and TV Shows. The merged dataset contains 10 columns and 2984 entries, spanning from 2008 to 2021. It provides a comprehensive overview of Netflix's content offerings during this period.

To better understand the dataset, we organize the features into the following key groups based on their relevance and role:

Content Metadata

title: Name of the movie or TV show.
type: Specifies if the entry is a movie or TV show.
listed_in: Genre(s) or categories the title falls under.
rating: Age-based content rating (e.g., PG, TV-MA).

Production & Release Details

release_year: Year the content was originally released.
country: Country of production.
date_added: When the title was made available on Netflix.

Performance & Engagement

imdb_score: IMDb user rating (0–10 scale).
imdb_votes: Number of user votes submitted to IMDb.

Runtime Information

runtime: Duration of a movie or average episode length for a TV show.

Distribution-Based Insights

These focus on specific characteristics of IMDb scores and provide a deeper dive into the data.

IMDb Scores by Media Runtime

This interactive boxplot visualizes the distribution of IMDb scores for TV shows and movies, categorized by runtime length. Hover over each box to learn more about the category and use the dropdown to filter between media categories. The runtime categories are:

  • Short: Runtime ≤ 90 minutes
  • Medium: Runtime ≤ 150 minutes
  • Long: Runtime > 150 minutes

Takeaways:

TV Shows

TV shows generally receive higher IMDb scores than movies across similar length categories. The median score for TV shows is approximately 7.0, with medium-length shows performing the best, reaching a median of around 7.5. This score is also matched by the single long TV show in the dataset, although it's important to note the very limited sample size in that category. TV shows tend to have a tighter distribution of core scores, indicating more consistent quality, especially in the medium-length segment. However, short TV shows do show more low-end outliers, suggesting higher variability in quality among shorter series.

Movies

Movies exhibit more variability in performance and tend to score slightly lower on average. Their median IMDb scores typically range between 6.2 and 6.8. There is a mild positive correlation between length and score—longer films tend to receive higher ratings than shorter ones. Long movies, in particular, display the smallest variation in scores, pointing to a more consistent quality level in that category. On the other hand, short and medium-length movies have a wider range, including low-end outliers scoring between 1.5 and 3.0. Medium-length movies form the largest sample group (n = 1376), giving more weight to observations in that category.

Overall, TV shows tend to outperform movies in terms of median IMDb scores, particularly in the medium-length category. While both content types share a similar overall score range (typically between 5 and 9), TV shows generally exhibit more consistent performance. The greater variability in movies—especially among shorter formats—suggests a wider range of production quality. These patterns highlight the potential value in prioritizing mid-length TV content for high audience satisfaction, while also being mindful of outlier risks in shorter productions.

Implications:

TV shows generally receive higher IMDb scores than movies, particularly medium-length series. For Netflix, this suggests a continued emphasis on TV series development, especially medium-length ones, as they seem to offer the best opportunity for positive viewer feedback and higher engagement. Investing in high-quality TV shows may help drive user retention and attract new subscribers.

Medium-length TV shows have the highest median scores, which means they strike the right balance between viewer engagement and content depth. For Netflix, focusing on this format could be a strategic move, ensuring high satisfaction while keeping viewers interested over several episodes. This could also help in securing more critical acclaim and audience loyalty.

Movies, particularly shorter ones, show a higher degree of variability with more low-end outliers in ratings. For Netflix, this could mean that shorter films might be more prone to disappointing viewers. Netflix might want to be more selective about the short films they produce or acquire, ensuring that they meet high-quality standards to avoid negative reviews and maximize success.

Audience Engagement and Popularity

In this section, we will analyze the relationship between IMDb scores and audience engagement metrics, specifically focusing on the number of votes received. This analysis will help us understand how different content types resonate with viewers and how age ratings impact audience engagement.

Average IMDb Score by Age Rating

The aim of this section of our analysis is to gain insight into the relationship between the age rating on content and viewer engagement. The figure is a horizontal bar chart with color encoding. Each bar represents the average IMDb score of titles within a specific age rating, while the bar color reflects the total number of votes received — one possible measure of popularity. Hover over the bars to see the metrics.

Takeaways

As the level of maturity increases, the amount of votes on that level of content is greater. This is the case until the maturity level R. NC-17 has significantly fewer votes than the rest of the age ratings due to the age certifications being far less popular than the rest listed.

Average score is fairly consistent across the various age certifications, despite the frequency of votes all being greatly different.

Content rated TV-Y, TV-Y7, TV-G, and G tend to have fewer total votes and slightly lower average scores. This potentially means less IMDb user engagement with content aimed at very young viewers — perhaps due to IMDb’s adult-skewing user base.

NC-17 ratings have a small presence in Netflix content. This category appears with low scores and vote counts, indicating it may represent niche or less mainstream content with little access or appeal.

Implications:

Streaming platforms and production companies should consider the larger engagement seen with PG-13, R, and TV-MA rated content when planning releases or marketing strategies. These categories clearly resonate more with IMDb’s user base, suggesting adult-oriented content garners more interaction and discussion.

The lower vote counts for youth-rated content reflect IMDb’s likely adult-leaning user demographic. Platforms targeting families or younger audiences may find that IMDb ratings don't fully capture engagement from their core viewers and should rely on alternative metrics.

The sharp drop-off in votes for NC-17 content suggests a limited audience and potentially reduced discoverability. This could impact content placement decisions for media that falls into that rating, especially on mainstream platforms like Netflix.

Since age ratings correlate with vote volume, developers and researchers should be cautious using IMDb data as a proxy for popularity or quality across all age groups. The data may overrepresent certain segments (like adult viewers) and underrepresent others (like children or families).

While average score may be very similar, the popularity difference across maturity levels indicates that we should look further into the individual distributions for each age rating.

Conclusion

The analysis of IMDb ratings across various factors, including media length, Netflix's content strategy, maturity levels, and audience engagement, has revealed several valuable insights.

Key Findings

  • Media Length and Ratings:
    Runtime alone is not a strong predictor of audience reception. Short films exhibit broader rating variability, making them riskier investments, while longer films tend to receive more consistent scores. However, TV shows—particularly those of medium length—show the best balance between viewer engagement and content quality, generally outperforming movies in terms of IMDb scores.
  • Netflix’s Content Strategy:
    Netflix has transitioned from acquiring highly-rated content to focusing on original programming. The platform’s emphasis on TV series, particularly medium-length ones, has proven to be more successful, offering better viewer retention and satisfaction. The rise in TV show ratings suggests that investing further in this format, while refining movie acquisitions, could enhance Netflix's content strategy.
  • Maturity Ratings and Audience Engagement:
    PG-13, R, and TV-MA content consistently receive the highest engagement on IMDb, with the number of reviews and votes serving as indicators of popularity and audience interaction. Conversely, youth-oriented and NC-17 content tend to have lower engagement, likely due to IMDb’s adult-skewed user base. Platforms targeting younger audiences may need to rely on alternative engagement metrics for more accurate assessments.
  • Ratings and Review Volume:
    High-rated content tends to generate more reviews, particularly for well-known or widely appreciated films and TV shows. Niche content, although highly rated, often receives fewer reviews, indicating limited audience reach. Streaming platforms could capitalize on high-rated but low-review content through targeted marketing to increase visibility and engagement.
  • Trends Over Time:
    IMDb rating fluctuations reflect industry shifts, the growth of streaming platforms, and changing audience preferences. The number of releases per year correlates with the platform’s growth and significant entertainment trends, like the rise of streaming and global events (e.g., the pandemic). Platforms could analyze these trends to inform their release strategies and adjust content offerings accordingly.

Limitations

  • IMDb’s user base may not fully represent all audience segments, particularly for family-friendly or niche content.
  • Regional differences, which could influence rating trends based on cultural preferences, are not accounted for in the current analysis.
  • Correlation does not imply causation, meaning that high ratings do not necessarily lead to high engagement or vice versa.
  • Additional quality metrics, such as audience retention, social media engagement, and sentiment analysis of reviews, are not included in this study.

Future Recommendations

  • Balance Quality and Quantity:
    Streaming platforms should strike a balance between quality and quantity, using data-driven insights to guide content acquisition and production strategies. Platforms can refine their focus to ensure content consistency while adapting to evolving viewer preferences.
  • Target Marketing for Low-Review Content:
    High-rated but low-review content presents an opportunity for targeted marketing campaigns. Platforms should increase awareness of these titles to expand their audience reach and improve engagement.
  • Incorporate Additional Engagement Metrics:
    Future analyses could benefit from incorporating alternative engagement metrics, such as watch time, social media mentions, and review sentiment. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of content success beyond ratings and votes.
  • Segment by Geographic Region:
    To better cater to diverse global audiences, segmenting data by geographic region could help platforms tailor content strategies, ensuring that culturally relevant content resonates well with specific viewer demographics.
  • Continuous Monitoring of Industry Trends:
    Regularly monitoring shifting industry trends and audience behaviors is crucial for adapting to technological advancements in the entertainment landscape. Platforms should remain flexible and agile, adjusting their strategies to ensure long-term success and audience satisfaction.

By leveraging these insights, content creators and streaming platforms can optimize their strategies, sustain long-term success, and maximize audience engagement, ensuring that their content offerings continue to meet the ever-evolving demands of viewers.
